About Us

The S.T.O.P B.A.D Program stands for Stand Together, Overcome the Pain against Bullying and Discrimination that represents an actionable plan to raise awareness of where to seek help and whom to approach, lessen the cases and to stand together to overcome the pain against bullying and discrimination. The program is a proposed localized, student-led, and comprehensive implementation for Commonwealth High School that will be conducted to take part of the effort and support the Quezon City Government’s Bullying and Discrimination, BAD ‘YAN! Program.

Our Mission

The S.T.O.P. B.A.D. Program (Stand Together, Overcome the Pain against Bullying and Discrimination) is committed to creating a bullying-free and inclusive school environment at Commonwealth High School by raising awareness on who to approach and seek help when witnessing or experiencing bullying and discrimination through school-wide symposiums and providing 7 executable S.T.O.P. B.A.D. framework steps.

Our Vision

Make Commonwealth High School a place where every student feels safe, seen, respected, and valued. In general, make CHS a free from bullying and discrimination campus where everyone can freely express themselves where mutual support, empathy and respect resonates to the school.
