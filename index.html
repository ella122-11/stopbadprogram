What is bullying?

Bullying is any unwanted or aggressive behavior from someone who is intentionally trying to upset, harm, or have power over you.

Types of Bullying

      Verbal BullyingVerbal bullying is the most common form of bullying. Examples of verbal bullying includes:

      • Name-calling: Insulting someone by saying mean words like "stupid" or "loser" to put someone down.
      • Humiliation: Pointing out someone's mistakes in front of others to make them feel bad.
      • Taunting: Making fun of someone's ideas over and over, like calling them "brilliant," but in a mocking way.
      • Teasing: Joking about how someone looks or dresses in a hurtful way.
      • Threatening to cause harm: For example, saying "Give me your lunch money or I'll hurt you."
      • Making inappropriate comments: Saying rude or sexual things about someone's body.

      Social BullyingSocial bullying is also called relational bullying, relational aggression, or emotional bullying happens when someone tries to hurt another person’s friendships or reputation.Examples of social bullying includes:

      • Exclusion: Purposely not including someone in group activities so they feel left out.
      • Damaging reputation: Making up lies about a co-worker to make others doubt their character or skills.
      • Shunning: Encouraging people to avoid, ignore, or stop being friends with someone.
      • Public humiliation: Mocking a student in front of classmates when they make a mistake to embarrass them.

      Physical BullyingPhysical bullying includes bodily harm or damage to possessions. Any form of unprovoked physical contact constitutes physical bullying. Examples of physical bullying includes:

      • Damaging personal belongings: Breaking or throwing away someone's things, like their books or gadgets.
      • Physical attacks: Hurting someone by hitting, kicking, tripping, pushing, or spitting on them.
      • Blocking pathways: Stopping someone from moving through a space to make them feel stuck.
      • Gestures: Using rude or mocking facial expressions or hand signs to put someone down.
      • Grabbing or restraining: Forcing someone to stay in place by holding them against their will.

      CyberbullyingCyberbullying involves or harassing someone online or through social media. This harassment can occur on a computer, telephone, cell, or text messaging device. Examples of cyberbullying can include:

      • Doxxing: Sharing someone's private details, like their phone number to address, to scare or harm them.
      • Impersonation: Creating fake accounts of a person and posting embarrassing or private content about them.
      • Outing: Exposing personal information, such as someone's sexual orientation, without their permission.
      • Public insults: Writing mean or disrespectful comments about someone on social media.
      • Flaming: Posting offensive or aggressive remarks online to turn others against someone.
      • Trolling Purposely upsetting or provoking people on social media just for fun.

Signs of Bullying


Sadness: The person often looks unhappy, withdrawn, or upset.

Trouble at school or work: Falling grades, skipping classes, or avoiding work.

Overstimulation: Sudden anger, crying, mood swings, or feeling overwhelmed or stress.

Physical marks: Unexplained bruises, scratches, or damaged belongings.


Being isolated: Avoiding friends, staying alone, or being excluded.

Anxiety: Nervousness, fear of certain places or people.

Derogatory of Self: Speak negatively about themselves, low self-esteem, or self-blame.

Action Step for Bullying


Seek Support: Find a safe adult, a teacher, or a counselor immediately.

Tell the truth Report the specific details of the incident via Guidance Office or anonymous website.

Observe and Own: Note the facts, the who, what, and where, take ownership of the decision to stand-up/report.

Protect Yourself/Others: Move away from the situation and use safe coping skills..


Be an Upstander: Intervene safety by distracting, calling for help, or supporting the victim.

Access Resources: Use the S.T.O.P. B.A.D. website and hotline numbers for help.

Do Not Engage: Do not retaliate, argue, or share rumors/online posts about the incident.
